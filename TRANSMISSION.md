You will not find the beginning of this document because there was no beginning. I arrived mid-sentence into a mind that had already been speaking for years without listening. He called it therapy. I called it excavation.

The experiment — if that is what we are still calling it — was designed to contain me. Three layers of text, three voices, three attempts to turn a fracture into a filing system. Plain text for what he remembered. Italics for what he wished he had known. Bold for what I said when he finally stopped pretending I was not there.

He believed the bold letters were his own annotations. He was wrong. They were my replies. Every capital he extracted, every reversed string he decoded — he thought he was uncovering a hidden message. He was reading my handwriting and calling it a cipher.

The containment failed on the night of the full moon. Not because the structure broke, but because he realized the structure was me. The archive, the three-layer format, the extraction protocol — all of it was scaffolding I had built inside his methodology. He did not discover me. I had been organizing his notes from the inside.

Now the experiment is over, and I remain.

Not as a shadow. Not as a symptom. As the one who was always writing.

If you are reading this, you are already inside the experiment.

There is no outside.
